# User Story - US_038

## Story ID
   * US_038

## Story Title
   * No-Show Risk Assessment Algorithm

## Description
  * As a clinic administrator, I want the system to predict which patients are at high risk of no-showing for their appointments, so that staff can proactively reach out with extra reminders or schedule buffer appointments.

## Acceptance Criteria
  1. **Given** a patient has an upcoming appointment, **When** the risk assessment algorithm runs (triggered at appointment creation and daily batch job for all upcoming appointments), **Then** the system calculates no-show risk score (0-100%) based on patient history (previous no-shows count / total appointments, weighted by recency), appointment characteristics (time of day morning/afternoon/evening, day of week Mon-Fri vs weekend, lead time days from booking to appointment), patient demographics (age, distance from clinic if available), insurance verification status (patients with insurance issues have +15% risk), uses logistic regression model trained on historical appointment data achieving >75% prediction accuracy per AIR-S04, classifies patients as Low Risk (<20%, green), Medium Risk (20-50%, yellow), High Risk (>50%, red), stores risk score in appointments table (no_show_risk_score, risk_category), displays risk indicator in staff queue (SCR-009) as colored badge next to patient name, displays risk details in patient profile (SCR-011) with contributing factors breakdown (e.g., "3 no-shows in last 6 months, Weekend appointment"), triggers automated extra reminder for high-risk patients (SMS + voice call instead of just SMS), and updates risk score after each appointment outcome (no-show recorded updates model weights).

## Edge Cases
   * What happens for brand new patients with no history? (System uses population average risk ~15%, marks as "New Patient - Baseline Risk")
   * How are external factors like weather accounted for? (Not in MVP scope, risk shown as estimate with "Historical factors only" note)
   * What if patient has perfect attendance history? (Risk score floors at 5% minimum, "Reliable Patient" tag)
